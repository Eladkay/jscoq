Welcome to the JsCoq Interactive System! Please bear with us as this software is pretty much a technology demo, and loading may take a bit. You must use a recent Chrome or Firefox, don't even try with old browsers :D!

Let's do a first proof in Coq!

A typical first proof in Coq is the commutativity of addition over natural numbers. In the standard library, it is quite straigforward, as you can see below.

Place the cursor somewhere below and press Ctrl-Enter to go to the point. Ctrl-N/P will move you throu the proof, F8 or the icon will hide/show the goal panel. We currently load 100MiB of libraries, so be patient the first time, you can see the progress by opening the right panel.

Below, you'll find a more interesting proof, the infinitude of primes, using the Mathematical Components libraries by MSR.

Note that the proof will load around 50 libraries, unfortunately ssrnat pulls half of the Coq standard library, so allow a few secons for the Require line to be processed.

Have fun!

The main proof relies in the `pdivP` lemma, that asserts that every number is divided by a prime (it actually picks the first prime divisor).

Now, we have proven that for any n, the first prime divisor of $!m + 1$ is greater than m. By contraposition, assume such prime is indeed less or equal than m, then it would contradict that p is a divisor of !m +1, completing the proof.
