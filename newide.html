The main proof relies in the `pdivP` lemma, that asserts that every number is divided by a prime (picking the first prime divisor actually).

We have proven that for any n, the first prime divisor of $!m + 1$ is greater than m. By contraposition, assume such prime is indeed less or equal than m, then it would contradict that p is a divisor of !m +1, completing the proof.
